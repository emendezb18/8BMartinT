Russian Civil War

At the outbreak of World War l, Russian Tsar Nicholas ll in 1914 had promised the conflict against central empires would be very short and that they would end up winning, However after the firsts confrontation the war extended and the problems started coming.


Russian soldiers tired of fighting started deserting the land, in March 1917 Tsar abdicated and was replaced by a socialist, Alexander Kerensky, this government also disappointed the country and in October of the same year the government was overthrown by a revolution led by the Bolsheviks


The Russian civil war started in 1917 on November 7 , the conflict consisted of the red army defending the newly formed Bolshevik Government led by Vladimir Lenin. During the war there was economic damage and a lot of food shortage, it was a massacre, more than 10 million people died during the clashes.


During the war the territory changed a lot




This war caused a lot of effects like:

The independence of Poland, Finland, Estonia, Lithuania and Latvia
The independence of Poland, Finland, Estonia, Lithuania and Latvia
The death of some 9 million people, which 1 million died from collateral damages such as the famines of 1921 to 1922
The exile of approximately one million Russians who escaped war, famine and political persecution.
